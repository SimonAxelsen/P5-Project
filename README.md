## Overview

"Et 'lille' problem" (A 'small' problem) is a VR simulation developed in collaboration with Aalborg University and Københavns Professionshøjskole. The experience places users in realistic school conflict scenarios, providing a safe environment to practice decision-making and reflection without real-world consequences.

## Concept

The simulation combines:
- **Branching narrative**: User decisions shape the story progression
- **Gestalt perception theory**: Environmental design based on psychological principles
- **Three-act structure**: Traditional storytelling framework adapted for interactive VR

## Research & Evaluation

The experience was tested with **15 participants** (pedagogues and pre-service teachers) at Københavns Professionshøjskole using mixed-methods methodology:

- **Quantitative**: Custom questionnaire with descriptive statistics and Pearson correlation analysis
- **Qualitative**: Thematic and keyword analysis of post-session interviews

### Key Findings

- High levels of perceived immersion and engagement
- Effective promotion of reflection on realistic school conflicts
- VR successfully bridges theoretical knowledge and practical application
- Narrative structure, reflective decision-making, and realistic design identified as core engaging elements

## Purpose

The project demonstrates VR's potential as an educational tool for teacher training, offering a foundation for innovative learning methods that combine immersive technology with pedagogical practice.

## Status

Completed as a semester project (P5) at Aalborg University (2024). Research findings indicate strong potential for VR-based teacher education.
